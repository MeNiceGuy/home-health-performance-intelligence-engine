## Executive Summary

Sample Care Agency faces significant operational and quality challenges that are directly impacting its HHVBP performance and financial sustainability. With a lean budget and moderate leadership readiness, the agency must prioritize stabilizing workforce coordination and patient intake processes to arrest quality leakage and improve reimbursement outcomes. Our recommendations focus on pragmatic, low-to-medium cost innovations that leverage existing technology and enhance staff engagement, enabling rapid operational improvements and measurable quality gains. This approach balances feasibility with impact, ensuring momentum without overextending limited resources.

## Strategic Priority Signal

**Rationale:**  
Delaying this focus risks continued staff burnout, scheduling errors, and slow patient onboarding—factors that directly increase readmissions and documentation delays. These issues depress quality scores and reimbursement, threatening financial viability under HHVBP.

## Priority Innovation Recommendations

### 1. Implement Advanced Scheduling and Communication Tools  
- **Why it fits:** Addresses high staff turnover and scheduling conflicts with low-cost, user-friendly software integrated with existing EHR/EVV.  
- **Operational impact:** Reduces scheduling errors by 30-50%, lowers burnout, and improves staff satisfaction.  
- **HHVBP/quality impact:** Improves visit timeliness and documentation accuracy, reducing readmissions by 10-15%.  
- **Cost level:** Low  
- **Risk level:** Low  
- **Time horizon:** 30-60 days  
- **Decision Score:** 9  
- **ROI potential:** 8  
- **Ease of implementation:** 9  
- **Speed to impact:** 8  

### 2. Optimize Patient Intake Workflow with Digital Pre-Admission Tools  
- **Why it fits:** Accelerates patient intake using digital forms and automated eligibility checks, reducing delays without heavy investment.  
- **Operational impact:** Cuts intake time by 25-40%, freeing staff capacity and improving patient experience.  
- **HHVBP/quality impact:** Faster intake supports timely care initiation, lowering readmission risk by ~10%.  
- **Cost level:** Medium  
- **Risk level:** Medium (requires staff training)  
- **Time horizon:** 60-90 days  
- **Decision Score:** 7  
- **ROI potential:** 7  
- **Ease of implementation:** 6  
- **Speed to impact:** 7  

### 3. Launch Targeted Documentation Improvement Program  
- **Why it fits:** Focuses on reducing documentation delays through staff training and workflow redesign, leveraging existing EHR capabilities.  
- **Operational impact:** Improves documentation timeliness by 20-30%, reducing compliance risk.  
- **HHVBP/quality impact:** Enhances quality reporting accuracy and supports better reimbursement.  
- **Cost level:** Low  
- **Risk level:** Low  
- **Time horizon:** 3-6 months  
- **Decision Score:** 6  
- **ROI potential:** 6  
- **Ease of implementation:** 7  
- **Speed to impact:** 5

## Priority Ranking

1. **Advanced Scheduling and Communication Tools** – Highest priority due to low cost, low risk, and rapid impact on workforce and quality issues.

## Leadership and Learning Considerations

Moderate leadership readiness and a developing learning culture present both opportunities and risks. Leadership must visibly champion these initiatives and allocate time for staff training and feedback loops. Encouraging a culture of continuous improvement will accelerate adoption and sustain gains. Underestimating the need for ongoing communication and reinforcement may hinder progress.

## Resource Allocation Guidance

- **Fund first:** Scheduling tool implementation and staff training – low cost, high impact, immediate relief.  
- **Delay:** Larger technology upgrades or new EHR modules until core workflows stabilize.  
- **Avoid:** High-cost, high-risk innovations that require significant capital or disrupt existing operations during this stabilization phase.
